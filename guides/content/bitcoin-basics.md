# Bitcoin Basics: Complete Beginner's Guide

Bitcoin is the world's first cryptocurrency, created in 2009 by an anonymous person or group using the pseudonym Satoshi Nakamoto. It's a decentralized digital currency that operates without a central bank or government control.

## What Makes Bitcoin Special?

**Decentralization**: No single entity controls Bitcoin. It runs on a network of thousands of computers worldwide.

**Limited Supply**: Only 21 million Bitcoin will ever exist, making it scarce like digital gold.

**Transparency**: All transactions are recorded on a public ledger called the blockchain.

**Security**: Cryptographic technology protects Bitcoin from fraud and counterfeiting.

## How Bitcoin Works

Bitcoin uses blockchain technology - a distributed ledger that records all transactions. When you send Bitcoin:

1. Your transaction is broadcast to the network
2. Network computers (miners) verify the transaction
3. The transaction is added to a block
4. The block is added to the blockchain permanently

## Getting Started with Bitcoin

**Step 1**: Choose a reputable cryptocurrency exchange
**Step 2**: Verify your identity and add payment method
**Step 3**: Buy Bitcoin with your local currency
**Step 4**: Transfer to a secure wallet for long-term storage

## Bitcoin Price Factors

Bitcoin's price is influenced by:
- Market demand and supply
- Institutional adoption
- Regulatory news
- Macroeconomic factors
- Technical developments

## Security Best Practices

- Use hardware wallets for large amounts
- Never share your private keys
- Enable two-factor authentication
- Keep software updated
- Use reputable exchanges only
